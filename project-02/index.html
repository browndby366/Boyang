What is Building Energy Rating

The Energy Star Rating, also known as an energy efficiency score, is that a building earns using the United States Environmental Protection Agency online benchmarking tool, Energy Star Portfolio Manager, to compare building energy performance to similar buildings in similar climates. EPA, in conjunctio n with stakeholders, developed this 1-100 ENERGY STAR score score2 as a screening tool, where the higher the score, the better the energy performance of the building.

As per Local Law 95 of 2019 grades based on Energy Star energy efficiency scores will be assigned as follows:

  • A – score is equal to or greater than 85
  • B – score is equal to or greater than 70 but less than 85
  • C – score is equal to or greater than 55 but less than 70
  • D – score is less than 55
  • F – for buildings that didn’t submit required benchmarking information
  • N – for buildings exempted from benchmarking or not covered by the Energy Star program
