Buiding Energy Efficiency Act in NYC

Buildings account for two-thirds of New York City’s GHG emissions. Most of the emissions come from electricity and fuel used for heating, cooling and power, and also from inefficient energy systems. According to data collected through the Greener, Greater Buildings Plan (GGBP), more than 80% of the heating systems in large buildings in New York City still use steam, which is very inefficient.

Energy efficiency is one of the simplest ways to eliminate energy waste and reduce energy cost. The City recognized that voluntary action was not sufficient，and issued the Climate Mobilization Act, which consists of climate laws aimed at significantly reducing New York City's carbon emissions.
